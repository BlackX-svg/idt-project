Mobile Legends

Released: July 14 2016

Developer: Moonton

Genre: MOBA, Competitive Multiplayer

Overview of Mobile Legends

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang is a fast‑paced 5v5 mobile MOBA released in 2016, built around quick matches, team strategy, and a large roster of heroes with unique roles. The game became massively popular during the 2020–2021 pandemic, often called its Golden Era, when player numbers, esports events, and community activity exploded. MLBB features classic MOBA elements—lanes, jungling, objectives, and teamfights—combined with simple controls and short match times that made it accessible worldwide. Over time, it expanded with new heroes, revamped maps, seasonal ranks, and major tournaments like the M‑Series, solidifying its place as one of the biggest mobile esports titles globally.

The Golden Era (2020–2021)

The years 2020–2021 are widely regarded as the Golden Era of Mobile Legends. During the global pandemic, MLBB became more than a game — it became a daily ritual, a social hub, and a competitive battleground for millions stuck at home. This era delivered the most iconic metas, the most memorable tournaments, and the highest surge in player activity the game has ever seen.

Why 2020–2021 Was Peak MLBB

  • Massive spike in global player count during lockdowns
  • Explosive growth of MPL and M2/M3 World Championships
  • Iconic hero metas that defined entire seasons
  • Frequent balance updates and hero reworks
  • Rise of legendary pro players and teams

Iconic Hero Metas of the Golden Era

The Golden Era was defined by fast‑paced, high‑damage metas that rewarded mechanical skill and aggressive playstyles. Many heroes reached their most iconic forms during this period.

  • Lancelot – The king of assassins during the pandemic meta.
  • Granger – The most dominant marksman of 2020.
  • Esmeralda – Unkillable shield queen.
  • Chou – Peak outplay potential.
  • Yu Zhong – The dragon that ruled sidelanes.

Peak Competitive Era

MLBB esports exploded during the Golden Era. With everyone at home, viewership numbers skyrocketed, and the MPL regions became global entertainment.

  • M2 World Championship – One of the most watched MLBB events ever.
  • MPL Indonesia S6–S7 – Peak rivalries and peak gameplay.
  • Rise of superteams – Bren, EVOS, RRQ, Blacklist.
  • Birth of iconic playstyles – UBE strategy, hypercarry meta.

Community & Cultural Impact

MLBB became a cultural phenomenon during the pandemic. It wasn’t just a game — it was a way for friends to stay connected, for communities to stay active, and for esports fans to stay entertained during global lockdowns.

  • Daily squads and ranked grinds became routine
  • Memes, streams, and TikTok clips exploded
  • MLBB became the #1 mobile MOBA in Southeast Asia
  • Heroes and skins became cultural icons

Legacy of the Golden Era

Even as the game continues to evolve, the 2020–2021 era remains unmatched in nostalgia, community energy, and gameplay identity. It was the perfect storm of timing, updates, and global circumstances — a moment in gaming history that will never be replicated.
